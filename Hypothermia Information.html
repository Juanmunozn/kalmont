Signs and Stages

Hypothermia is a condition where the body loses heat faster than it can produce it, leading to a dangerously low core body temperature. Recognizing the signs and stages of hypothermia is crucial for prompt intervention and treatment. Here are the signs and stages:

Mild Hypothermia

(Core temperature 90-95°F or 32-35°C)

  • Shivering: The body's natural response to generate heat.
  • Cold extremities: Hands and feet may feel cold and numb.
  • Pale skin: Skin may appear pale or bluish due to reduced blood flow to the extremities.
  • Slurred speech: Speech may become slow or difficult to understand.
  • Difficulty performing fine motor tasks: Decreased dexterity and coordination.

Moderate Hypothermia

(Core temperature 82-90°F or 28-32°C)

  • Intensified symptoms: Shivering may become more intense initially but then decrease as the body loses its ability to generate heat.
  • Confusion and altered mental status: Disorientation, confusion, and impaired judgment.
  • Loss of coordination: Difficulty walking or standing, stumbling, and poor balance.
  • Slowed heart rate and breathing: Heart rate and breathing may become slow and irregular.

Severe Hypothermia

(Core temperature below 82°F or 28°C)

  • Medical emergency: Severe hypothermia is life-threatening and requires immediate medical attention.
  • Loss of consciousness: The person may become unconscious and unresponsive.
  • Weak or absent pulse: The pulse may be weak or difficult to detect.
  • Shallow or absent breathing: Breathing may be slow, shallow, or absent.
  • Dilated pupils: Pupils may be dilated and unreactive to light.
